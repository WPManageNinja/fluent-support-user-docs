# Internal Notifications

**Internal Notifications** help your support agents stay updated inside Fluent Support without depending only on email notifications. When this feature is enabled, agents can see a notification bell, unread counts, ticket activity updates, mentions, and automation-related notifications from the Fluent Support dashboard.

This is useful when your team works from the ticket dashboard all day and needs quick updates about assigned tickets, customer replies, ticket status changes, and workflow activity.

## When Should You Use Internal Notifications?

You can use Internal Notifications when you want your support team to:

* See important ticket updates directly inside Fluent Support.
* Quickly open tickets from notification alerts.
* Track unread updates with a notification badge.
* Separate notifications by mentions, ticket activity, and automation events.
* Reduce dependency on email inboxes for internal team updates.

## Enable Internal Notifications

To enable Internal Notifications, go to your **Fluent Support Dashboard** and open the settings area where your global business settings are managed.

Now, enable **Internal Notifications** and click **Save Settings**.

Once enabled, Fluent Support will show the notification bell in the dashboard for agents.

::: info
Notification data older than 15 days is removed automatically to keep the notification list clean.
:::

## Use the Notification Bell

After Internal Notifications are enabled, agents will see a **Notification Bell** in the Fluent Support dashboard. If there are unread notifications, the bell will show an unread count.

<!-- TODO: Capture screenshot for this step and save it at /images/ticket-management/daily-operations/internal-notifications/notification-bell.webp -->

Click the bell icon to open the **Unread Notifications** panel. From this panel, you can:

* View recent unread notifications.
* Filter notifications by category.
* Open the related ticket by clicking a notification.
* Mark a single notification as read.
* Click **Mark all as read** to clear all unread notifications.
* Open **Notification Settings** from the settings icon if you have permission to manage settings.

## Notification Categories

Internal Notifications are grouped into categories so agents can focus on the updates they need.

* **All:** Shows every available notification.
* **Mentions:** Shows notifications when an agent is mentioned in a reply or internal note.
* **Ticket Activity:** Shows updates such as ticket assignment, replies, closing, and reopening.
* **Automation Triggers:** Shows notifications related to workflow activity.

Mention notifications are always enabled, so agents can receive updates when another team member mentions them in a ticket conversation or internal note.

## View All Notifications

From the notification panel, click **View All** to open the full **Notifications** page.

On this page, agents can:

* Filter notifications by category.
* Enable **Unread only** to see only unread notifications.
* Refresh the notification list.
* Open ticket-related notifications.
* Navigate through older notifications using pagination.

## Manage Notification Preferences

Admins and users with settings permission can open **Notification Settings** from the notification panel.

From here, you can control which internal notification types are enabled for your team.

Available preferences include:

* **Assignment Notifications:** Notify agents when tickets are assigned or reassigned.
* **Customer Reply Notifications:** Notify the assigned agent when a customer replies.
* **Workflow Notifications:** Notify agents when workflow activity is triggered.
* **Ticket Status Notifications:** Notify agents when tickets are closed or reopened.
* **Agent Reply Notifications:** Notify the assigned agent when another agent replies.
* **Self Notifications:** Allow agents to receive notifications for their own actions when they are already a normal recipient.

::: tip
By default, agents are not notified for their own actions. Enable **Self Notifications** only if your team needs agents to receive those updates too.
:::

## Difference Between Internal and Email Notifications

Internal Notifications appear inside the Fluent Support dashboard for agents. Email Notifications are sent through email based on your inbox notification settings.

If you want to customize customer or agent email templates, check the [Managing Email Notifications](/customize-email-notifications) documentation.

Internal Notifications are best for team activity inside the dashboard, while Email Notifications are best for sending updates to inboxes.
